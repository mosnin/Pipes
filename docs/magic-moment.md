# Magic moment

This is the 30 seconds we sell to the staff engineer shipping a multi-agent system to production who is tired of pasting architecture into every new chat. The headline above the scene is "One map your team and your agents both read." This single scene defines the homepage hero, the empty editor, the demo video, and the launch tweet.

## The scene - 30 seconds, beat by beat

1. The engineer opens an empty Pipes canvas and sees one line: "One map your team and your agents both read."
2. They press slash, pick "Agent" from the palette, and a node lands on the canvas with a blinking title.
3. They type one sentence into the node: "Planner agent. Reads tickets, writes a plan, hands off to the coder."
4. They click the one button in the top right that they will remember afterward: Connect Agent.
5. A panel slides in with a Bearer token and a Claude Desktop config block already filled in for this system; they copy the block, paste it once into their Claude config, and reopen Claude.
6. They ask Claude "what is in my Pipes system?" and Claude answers by name: one Agent node titled Planner agent, with the sentence they wrote, fetched live through the MCP endpoint - they pasted no architecture.

The one click they remember is Connect Agent.

## The frame

The hero screenshot is a split frame at the instant beat 6 lands. Left half: the Pipes canvas with exactly one node, titled "Planner agent," with the one-sentence description visible. Right half: a Claude chat window where Claude has just replied, quoting the node title and description back, with a small "via pipes (MCP)" tool-call chip above its message. Not in the frame: no inspector tabs, no node palette, no second node, no pipes between nodes, no customer logos, no pricing, no Pipes nav chrome beyond the system name in the top left.

## The line

"It already knows my system."

## What this scene replaces

- The homepage hero illustration and copy.
- The protocol page hero and the use-cases page hero.
- The empty editor canvas placeholder and prompt.
- The dashboard empty state when a workspace has zero systems.
- The docs landing illustration.
- The first frame of the demo video and the still in the launch tweet.

## What this scene refuses

- Showing more than one node, or any pipe, in the screenshot.
- Showing the inspector, the node-type grid, or the validation panel.
- Showing the four-step token wizard - we show the token and the config block, nothing else.
- Showing a customer logo strip, a price, or a feature comparison anywhere in the frame.
- A demo video longer than 30 seconds or with a voiceover that explains what MCP is.
- Any copy that uses the words platform, solution, seamless, or unlock.
